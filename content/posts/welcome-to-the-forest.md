# Welcome to the Forest – A First Story

This is a simple example of the kind of story we hope to collect and share in PaFoI.

It is here to:

- Show how a story can be short and honest.  
- Give you a sense of tone: gentle, real, and hopeful.  
- Act as a template for your own stories.

---

## Before: feeling stuck and unsure

I had been feeling strangely flat for a while.

On paper, things looked fine: I had a roof over my head, food on the table, and people around me. But inside, I felt under‑used and a little invisible. I spent a lot of time scrolling, watching other people’s lives, and telling myself I would do something “later”.

Part of me thought:

- “Maybe this is just how things are now.”  
- “I don’t have big ideas anymore.”  
- “Other people are doing important things; I’m not.”

Then a friend sent me a link to **Plant a Forest of Ideas**.

---

## The tiny seed

On the PaFoI site, there was a question:

> “If you had a little more energy and support, what is one thing you’d be curious to explore?”

I didn’t know the answer immediately. But I wrote down a small seed:

> “I’d like to meet people who also feel a bit stuck, and see if we can encourage each other.”

It felt almost too simple, but it was honest.

That one sentence became my **Idea Seed**.

---

## The first small step

With a bit of help from a PaFoI Companion, we turned that seed into a tiny experiment:

- Invite 3–5 people to a short online circle.  
- Meet just three times, not “forever”.  
- Use a few gentle questions to guide us.

The experiment was clear and small enough that I didn’t feel overwhelmed.

We called it the **“Little Steps Circle.”**

---

## What happened

Not everything was smooth. Two people couldn’t make it to all the sessions. Sometimes I worried I was talking too much, or not enough.

But over three meetings:

- I realised I wasn’t the only one who felt this way.  
- I saw that my story made others feel less alone.  
- I noticed sparks of ideas coming alive in people’s faces.

Someone decided to start a weekly walk with a neighbour. Another person began sketching again after years of not drawing. I found myself looking forward to our calls.

The circle may or may not continue, but something in me shifted.

---

## What I learned

A few things I took from this:

- I don’t need a huge, polished idea to begin.  
- A very small, time‑bound experiment is enough to change how I feel.  
- Listening to others’ stories is as powerful as sharing my own.  
- I am not as alone or “behind” as I thought.

Most importantly: **the feeling of being stuck is real, but it is not the whole story.**

---

## If this resonates with you

If any part of this sounds familiar:

- You’re not alone.  
- You don’t have to fix everything at once.  
- You can start with one small seed, just like I did.

You might:

- Answer one of the prompts on the **Start** page.  
- Join a circle that fits where you are.  
- Or simply write your own seed in a notebook and hold it gently for now.
