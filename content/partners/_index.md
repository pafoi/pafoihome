# Partners

Plant a Forest of Ideas (PaFoI) is strongest when it connects with other people and places that care about how we live, learn, and thrive.

This page is for **schools, organisations, community spaces, networks, and initiatives** who want to grow the forest with us.

---

## Who we partner with

We are especially keen to work with:

- **Schools and universities**  
  - Primary, secondary, and tertiary institutions  
  - Co‑curricular and enrichment programmes  
  - Educator networks and teacher training bodies

- **Community spaces and organisations**  
  - Libraries, community centres, neighbourhood hubs  
  - NGOs focused on wellbeing, livelihoods, or inclusion  
  - Faith‑based and civic groups

- **Learning and training providers**  
  - Adult learning centres and skills programmes  
  - Corporate L&D, leadership programmes  
  - Ed‑tech and learning designers

- **Groups in transition and support**  
  - Organisations working with retirees and seniors  
  - Career transition centres and retrenchment support  
  - Caregiver and family support organisations

- **Social ventures and local initiatives**  
  - Community organisers and social entrepreneurs  
  - Small ventures experimenting with new ways of working and living

If your work touches on **human flourishing, learning, community, or future‑ready livelihoods**, there is likely a way we can collaborate.

---

## How PaFoI can support you

We bring:

- **Formats and tools**  
  - Idea dialogues, circles, and labs you can host in your space.  
  - Simple templates for idea development, projects, and reflection.

- **Community and cross‑pollination**  
  - Access to a mix of retirees, mid‑career folks, caregivers, students, and educators.  
  - Opportunities to connect your participants with others beyond your walls.

- **Sense‑making and documentation**  
  - Help turning what happens in your programmes into stories, patterns, and insights.  
  - Seasonal Forest Reports and learning notes you can use in your own planning.

We are not here to replace your work, but to **weave it into a wider forest**.

## Ways we can work together

### 1. Co‑host circles and dialogues

We can co‑design and co‑host:

- “Plant a Forest of Ideas” dialogues for your community.  
- Circles for specific groups (retirees, students, caregivers, people in transition).  
- Themed sessions around questions that matter in your context.

You provide the people and place (physical or online). We bring formats, facilitation, and follow‑up.

[Talk about co‑hosting a circle](#)

---

### 2. Integrate PaFoI into learning programmes

For schools, universities, and learning providers, we can:

- Embed idea planting and project‑based learning into existing modules.  
- Create small PaFoI “labs” for students, educators, or participants.  
- Support reflection and documentation so learning is visible and shareable.

This can be as small as a single lesson, or as substantial as a term‑length project.

[Explore learning partnerships](#)

---

### 3. Support people in transition and care

If you work with:

- Retirees, mid‑career switchers, or retrenched workers  
- Caregivers and stay‑at‑home parents  
- Youth and young adults in uncertain times

we can:

- Offer gentle circles and idea‑based activities as part of your support services.  
- Provide tools that help people turn feelings and questions into small experiments.  
- Share what we learn back with you so services can adapt.

[Explore transition & care collaborations](#)

### 4. Share stories and patterns

We can collaborate on:

- **Stories** – highlighting journeys from your community within our “Stories from the forest” series.  
- **Publications** – co‑writing short reports or reflections based on joint work.  
- **Events** – co‑hosting gatherings where different communities learn from each other.

This helps your community’s experience influence wider conversations about how we live and learn.

## What we ask from partners

We look for partners who:

- Respect people’s dignity, time, and emotional realities.  
- Are open to learning and adjusting as we go.  
- Are comfortable with a **commons mindset** – allowing ideas and patterns to be shared (with credit) so others can benefit.

We also ask for:

- A named contact person who cares about the collaboration.  
- A willingness to start small, learn, and grow if it makes sense.

---

## How to start a conversation

You don’t need a fully formed proposal. A simple “this is who we are and what we’re wondering about” is enough.

We suggest:

1. **Introduce your organisation or group**  
   - Who you are, who you serve, and what you care about.

2. **Share what’s alive for you right now**  
   - A challenge, question, or group you’d like to support differently.

3. **Name what you imagine**  
   - A rough idea of how PaFoI might help, or simply a desire to explore together.
